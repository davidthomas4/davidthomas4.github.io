Currently (2017-), I am a PhD student at Lancaster University in the Condensed Matter Theory group under the supervision of Neil Drummond, and a member of the Graphene NOWNANO CDT. I use quantum Monte Carlo methods to study the properties of low-dimensional materials, such as quantum rings and graphene.

Previously, (2013-2017) I was an undergraduate at Lancaster University and earned a first class MSci degree in Theoretical Physics with Mathematics.
